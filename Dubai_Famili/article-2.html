Planning Your First Family Trip to Dubai

December 2024

New to Dubai? Our comprehensive guide covers everything you need to know for your first family adventure. Tips on weather, best times to visit, and must-see attractions to make your trip unforgettable.

Best Time to Visit

Dubai's climate is hot year-round, but the best time for families is from November to March when temperatures are more comfortable (20-30°C). This is peak tourist season, so book accommodations and attractions in advance. Summer months (June-September) are extremely hot but offer lower prices and fewer crowds.

Getting Around

Dubai has an excellent public transport system including the Metro, buses, and taxis. For families, consider renting a car for flexibility, especially if visiting multiple attractions. The Dubai Metro is stroller-friendly and connects major attractions. Taxis are affordable and widely available.

Accommodation Tips

Choose family-friendly hotels with amenities like pools, family lounges, and connecting rooms. Areas like Dubai Marina, JBR, and Downtown Dubai offer easy access to attractions. Many hotels provide family packages and special rates. Consider serviced apartments for longer stays with kitchen facilities.

What to Pack

Pack lightweight, breathable clothing for hot weather, but also bring layers for air-conditioned malls and indoor attractions. Don't forget sunscreen (SPF 50+), hats, sunglasses, and reusable water bottles. Comfortable walking shoes are essential. For visits to mosques, bring modest clothing that covers shoulders and knees.

Cultural Considerations

Dubai is very family-friendly and welcoming to tourists. Public displays of affection should be minimal. Dress modestly when visiting religious sites. Alcohol is available in licensed venues. Friday is the holy day, so some attractions may have different hours. Respect local customs and traditions.

Budget Planning

Dubai can be expensive, but there are ways to save. Look for combo tickets and family packages at theme parks. Many attractions offer family discounts and special rates. Dining options range from budget-friendly food courts to fine dining. Consider purchasing a Dubai Pass for discounted entry to multiple attractions.

Safety & Health

Dubai is one of the safest cities in the world with low crime rates. Tap water is safe to drink. Medical facilities are excellent. Bring any prescription medications you need. Travel insurance is recommended. Emergency services can be reached at 999.

First-Time Visitor Checklist

  • Book flights and hotels 2-3 months in advance for best prices
  • Purchase attraction tickets online to skip queues
  • Download useful apps: Dubai Metro, Careem/Uber, Dubai Calendar
  • Exchange currency or use ATMs (widely available)
  • Check visa requirements for your nationality
  • Pack appropriate clothing for weather and cultural sites
  • Plan your itinerary but allow flexibility for spontaneous discoveries
