Safety Tips for Family Adventures

December 2024

Keep your family safe while exploring Dubai's attractions. Learn about height requirements, safety measures, and what to pack for a worry-free adventure.

Height Requirements & Age Restrictions

Most theme parks have strict height requirements for rides. Check your family members' heights before visiting and check attraction websites for specific requirements. Many parks offer "rider swap" programs allowing family members to take turns on rides. Always follow height and safety recommendations for activities.

Sun Protection

Dubai's sun is intense year-round. Apply SPF 50+ sunscreen every 2 hours for all family members. Wear wide-brimmed hats, UV-protective clothing, and sunglasses. Seek shade during peak sun hours (11 AM - 3 PM). Many outdoor attractions have shaded areas and misting systems. Stay hydrated with water, not sugary drinks.

Water Safety

At water parks and beaches, always supervise family members closely, especially non-swimmers. Use life jackets for non-swimmers. Follow lifeguard instructions and stay within designated swimming areas. Be aware of rip currents at beaches. Many water parks provide free life jackets. Never swim alone, even in shallow areas.

Hydration & Heat Safety

Dehydration is a real risk in Dubai's heat. Drink water regularly, even if not thirsty. Carry reusable water bottles and refill at water stations available throughout attractions. Watch for signs of heat exhaustion: dizziness, nausea, excessive sweating. Take breaks in air-conditioned areas. Dress in light, breathable fabrics.

Crowd Safety

Popular attractions can get crowded, especially during peak season. Stay together in crowded areas. Establish a meeting point if separated. Consider using safety wristbands with contact information for all family members. Many parks offer "lost person" services. Keep recent photos of family members on your phone.

What to Pack

Essential items: sunscreen, hats, sunglasses, water bottles, hand sanitizer, wet wipes, first aid kit, snacks, extra clothes, and any necessary medications. For theme parks, bring comfortable walking shoes, a stroller for toddlers, and a portable phone charger. Keep important documents and emergency contacts easily accessible.

Emergency Preparedness

Save emergency numbers: 999 for police/ambulance, your country's embassy, and your hotel's front desk. Know the location of first aid stations at attractions. Keep a list of allergies and medical conditions for each family member. Have travel insurance information readily available. Establish a plan for what to do if family members get separated.

Food Safety

Dubai has excellent food safety standards. However, be cautious with street food if family members have sensitive stomachs. Stick to reputable restaurants and avoid raw or undercooked foods when needed. Many attractions have family-friendly dining options. Check for food allergies when ordering. Carry snacks for convenience.

Safety Checklist

  • Check height requirements before visiting attractions
  • Pack sunscreen, hats, and protective clothing
  • Bring plenty of water and stay hydrated
  • Establish meeting points in case of separation
  • Save emergency contact numbers
  • Carry first aid kit and necessary medications
  • Supervise family members at all times, especially near water
  • Follow all safety instructions and ride restrictions
