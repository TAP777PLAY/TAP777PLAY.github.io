The Olympian Pantheon

Discover the twelve great gods and goddesses who ruled from Mount Olympus

The Twelve Olympians

Zeus

King of the Gods

▼

The ruler of Mount Olympus and god of the sky, lightning, and thunder. He is the father of gods and men, known for his power and authority over all other deities.

Hera

Queen of the Gods

▼

Goddess of marriage, women, and childbirth. She is Zeus's wife and sister, known for her jealousy and protective nature over married women.

Poseidon

God of the Sea

▼

Ruler of the seas, earthquakes, and horses. Brother of Zeus and one of the most powerful Olympians, known for his trident and tempestuous nature.

Athena

Goddess of Wisdom

▼

Virgin goddess of wisdom, warfare, and crafts. She is the patron of Athens and a skilled strategist, born from Zeus's head fully grown and armored.

Apollo

God of the Sun

God of music, poetry, prophecy, and healing. He drives the sun chariot across the sky each day.

Artemis

Goddess of the Hunt

Virgin goddess of the hunt, wilderness, and childbirth. She is Apollo's twin sister and a skilled archer.

Ares

God of War

God of war, courage, and bloodshed. He represents the violent and physical aspect of warfare.

Aphrodite

Goddess of Love

Goddess of love, beauty, and desire. She was born from the sea foam and is the most beautiful of all goddesses.

Hephaestus

God of Fire

God of fire, metalworking, and craftsmanship. He is the smith of the gods and creates their weapons and armour.

Hermes

Messenger of the Gods

God of trade, thieves, and travel. He is the messenger of the gods and guide of souls to the underworld.

Dionysus

God of Wine

God of wine, fertility, and theatre. He is the youngest Olympian and brings joy and madness to mortals.
