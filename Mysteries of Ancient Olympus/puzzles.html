The Riddle of the Sphinx

Difficulty: Hard

▼

The Riddle:

"What walks on four legs in the morning, two legs at noon, and three legs in the evening?"

Answer: A human being

The riddle describes the stages of human life: crawling as a baby (four legs), walking upright as an adult (two legs), and using a cane in old age (three legs).

Athena's Wisdom Test

Difficulty: Expert

▼

The Challenge:

"Arrange the twelve Olympian gods in order of their importance to ancient Greek society. Consider their domains, influence, and relationships."

Answer: Zeus, Hera, Poseidon, Athena, Apollo, Artemis, Ares, Aphrodite, Hephaestus, Hermes, Dionysus, Hestia

This ranking considers both divine hierarchy and cultural significance in ancient Greek society.

Hermes' Messenger Puzzle

Difficulty: Medium

▼

The Challenge:

"You must deliver messages to all twelve Olympian gods. You can only visit each god once. What is the most efficient route that visits all gods and returns to Mount Olympus?"

Answer: This is a variation of the Traveling Salesman Problem

The optimal route would minimize travel time while ensuring all gods receive their messages. The solution depends on the specific locations and distances between divine domains.
